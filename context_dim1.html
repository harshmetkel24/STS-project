CONTEXT AND SITUATEDNESS

Where does it appear in the world?

Virtually everywhere in the world, across and among the countries. Migration due to prosecution, migration due to change of state control, migration for trade and migration due to pandemics and epidemics.

How does it appear and next to what or in what?

This is quite related to the cause of the appearance of the event. It begins as a change in the surroundings of the people. Gradually the condition worsens and people are forced to leave behind everything and go out to search for a living. It appears next to the vicious cycle of poverty and unemployment in the deprived regions of the world.

What kinds of audiences is it addressed to?

The major audience is the pool of daily-wage labourers. People who had left their towns for better prospects in some other cities.

Who is excluded in these addresses?

People who have economically stable and steady lives, have a safe job or business at their disposal are excluded.

When can it appear?

People migrate when they see that the environment can no longer sustain their daily basic requirements. It appears due to a large-scale shift in dynamics of the region â€“ be it political, social, economic or physiological. Shutdown of factories, fall of economy, toppling of government, rise of foreign power, inflation, pandemics and epidemics are the major reasons for the appearance of large-scale migration of people, workers in particular.

How does this matter?

This matters as it involves various dimensions of the society be it labour, material, technological, political, economic, bodily, historic, educational or symbolic. Migration changes the dynamics of places from where and the places to which people migrate. It can cause a chain reaction of developments in different areas which could affect the lives of a large number of people.
